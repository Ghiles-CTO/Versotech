# Lawyer Guide

Complete guide for lawyers - legal document review, certificate signing, and compliance support.

---

## Your Dashboard

As a lawyer, your dashboard shows:
- Pending documents for review
- Certificates awaiting signature
- Recent activity
- Vehicles/deals you're assigned to

---

## Lawyer Role

Lawyers on Versotech:
- Are assigned as service providers to vehicles
- Sign certificates as the first signatory
- May review legal documents
- Support compliance processes

---

## 1. Getting Onboarded

### Your Profile

Complete your profile:

1. **Profile** → **Edit Profile**
2. Required:
   - Full legal name
   - Firm name (if applicable)
   - Email
   - Phone
   - Bar admission / credentials

### Entity Setup

If representing a law firm:
- Firm must be registered as entity
- You're added as member/signatory
- Firm can be assigned to vehicles

## 2. Being Assigned to Vehicles

Lawyers are assigned at the **vehicle level** as a service provider.

### What This Means

When assigned to a vehicle:
- You're the lawyer for ALL deals in that vehicle
- Certificates route to you for signing
- You may access legal documents

### Viewing Your Assignments

**Vehicles** or **My Assignments**

See:
- Vehicles you're assigned to
- Active deals
- Pending actions

---

## 3. Certificate Signing

Your primary workflow is signing certificates after deals close.

### Certificate Purpose

Certificates confirm investor ownership:
- Number of units/shares
- Investment amount
- Date of investment
- Position in vehicle

### Signing Flow

```
Deal Closes
     │
     ▼
Certificates Generated (for each activated subscription)
     │
     ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ LAWYER SIGNS FIRST (Party A)        │
│                                     │
│ You receive signing notification    │
│ Review and sign certificate         │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
     │
     ▼
CEO Countersigns (Party B)
     │
     ▼
Certificate Complete → Sent to Investor
```

### Finding Pending Certificates

**My Tasks** → **Pending Signatures**

Or check email for signing links.

### Signing a Certificate

1. Click signing link or open from Tasks
2. Review certificate content:
   - Investor name correct
   - Units/shares correct
   - Amount correct
   - Deal/vehicle correct
3. Apply your signature
4. Submit

### Multi-Page Certificates

Some certificates have multiple pages:
- Signature fields pre-positioned
- May need signature on multiple pages
- Follow the prompts

## 6. Signature Process Details

### Receiving Signing Requests

When a certificate is ready:
1. Email notification sent
2. Contains signing link
3. Link valid for 24-48 hours

### Accessing the Signing Page

Click link or navigate via **My Tasks**

You'll see:
- Certificate document
- Your signature field highlighted
- Submit button

### Applying Signature

Options:
- Type your name (system generates signature)
- Draw signature (mouse/touchscreen)
- Upload signature image

### After Signing

- Certificate routes to CEO for countersignature
- When complete, investor receives it
- You see completion in history

## Troubleshooting

### "Signing link expired"

- Links expire after 24-48 hours
- Request resend from arranger
- Check **My Tasks** for fresh link

### "Can't find certificate"

- Check **My Tasks** → **Pending Signatures**
- Filter by document type
- Contact arranger if missing

### "Certificate has incorrect information"

DO NOT sign if information is wrong:
1. Note the issue
2. Contact arranger
3. Certificate will be regenerated with corrections

### "Too many certificates to sign"

After large deal closes:
- Many certificates may be pending
- Work through queue systematically
- Contact support if volume is excessive

## Best Practices

### Response Time
- Sign certificates promptly
- Investors waiting for their certificates
- Don't let queue build up

### Verification
- Review certificate content before signing
- Verify investor and deal details
- Report any discrepancies

### Record Keeping
- Note what you've signed
- Keep records per your firm requirements
- Export history if needed

### Communication
- Respond to queries promptly
- Flag issues early
- Coordinate with arranger on delays

## Key Workflows Summary

### After Deal Close

```
1. Deal closes
2. Subscriptions activated
3. Certificates generated
4. YOU receive signing requests
5. Review and sign each
6. Routes to CEO
7. CEO signs
8. Investor receives certificate
```

### Timeline

- Certificates generated: Within hours of close
- Your signature: ASAP (ideally same day)
- CEO signature: Typically same day
- Investor receipt: Within 1-2 days of close

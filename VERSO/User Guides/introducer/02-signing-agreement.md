# Introducer Guide: Signing Agreement

Learn how to sign the introducer agreement to formalize your relationship with an arranger.

---

## Overview

The **Introducer Agreement** is the foundational legal document establishing your relationship with an arranger. You must sign this before you can make introductions and earn commissions.

---

## Why an Agreement is Required

The agreement covers:
- Your role and responsibilities
- Commission arrangements
- Confidentiality obligations
- Compliance requirements
- Termination terms

---

## Receiving an Agreement

### How You're Notified

When an arranger adds you to their network:
1. **Email notification** with agreement link
2. **Platform notification** in your bell icon
3. **Dashboard** shows pending agreement

### What to Expect

The notification includes:
- Arranger name and company
- Agreement type
- Link to review and sign

---

## Reviewing the Agreement

### Step 1: Access the Agreement

From email:
- Click the signing link
- Opens in signing interface

From platform:
1. Go to **Documents** → **Pending**
2. Click on the agreement
3. Opens for review

### Step 2: Read Carefully

Key sections to review:

| Section | What It Covers |
|---------|----------------|
| **Definitions** | Key terms used |
| **Scope** | What you can/cannot do |
| **Commissions** | How compensation works |
| **Confidentiality** | Information handling |
| **Term** | Duration and renewal |
| **Termination** | How either party can end |

### Step 3: Note Key Terms

Pay attention to:
- Commission payment timing
- Exclusivity provisions (if any)
- Non-compete clauses
- Termination notice period

---

## Signing the Agreement

### Step 1: Prepare to Sign

Before signing:
- ✅ Read entire document
- ✅ Understand obligations
- ✅ Note any concerns

### Step 2: Apply Signature

1. Scroll to signature section
2. Click signature field
3. Choose method:
   - **Draw** - Write with mouse/finger
   - **Type** - Name converted to signature
   - **Upload** - Existing signature image
4. Click **"Apply"**

### Step 3: Complete Additional Fields

You may need to fill:
- Date
- Your name (printed)
- Title/capacity
- Company (if applicable)

### Step 4: Submit

1. Review all entries
2. Click **"Complete Signing"**
3. Agreement submitted
4. Confirmation email sent

---

## After Signing

### Immediate Effects

Once signed:
- Agreement status: `active`
- You're in arranger's network
- Can receive deal dispatches
- Can accept fee plans

### Document Access

Your signed agreement:
- Stored in **Documents** section
- Always accessible
- Reference for terms

---

## Agreement Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `pending` | Awaiting your signature |
| `active` | Signed and in effect |
| `expired` | Term ended |
| `terminated` | Relationship ended |

---

## Working with Multiple Arrangers

### Separate Agreements

Each arranger relationship requires:
- Separate agreement
- Separate fee plans per deal
- Independent tracking

### Managing Multiple

1. Go to **Documents**
2. See all agreements
3. Filter by arranger
4. Track status of each

---

## Agreement Renewal

### Expiring Agreements

If agreement has a term:
1. Notification sent before expiry
2. Arranger sends renewal
3. Sign to continue relationship

### Automatic Renewal

Some agreements auto-renew:
- Check your terms
- Notice if declining renewal

---

## Termination

### If You Want to End

To terminate your agreement:
1. Review termination clause
2. Provide required notice
3. Written notice typically required
4. Existing commissions honored

### If Arranger Terminates

You'll receive:
- Termination notice
- Final commission statement
- Payment timeline for pending

---

## Common Questions

### "Do I need a separate agreement for each deal?"

No. One agreement with an arranger covers all their deals. But you need separate **fee plans** per deal.

### "What if I disagree with some terms?"

Before signing:
1. Contact the arranger
2. Discuss your concerns
3. They may accommodate
4. Or clarify the terms

### "Can I sign for my company?"

If you're representing a company:
1. Ensure you have authority
2. Sign in your capacity
3. Company bound by agreement

---

## Best Practices

### Before Signing

- Read everything
- Ask questions if unclear
- Keep a copy for reference
- Note key dates (term, renewal)

### After Signing

- Store document safely
- Track agreement expiry
- Maintain compliance
- Update if circumstances change

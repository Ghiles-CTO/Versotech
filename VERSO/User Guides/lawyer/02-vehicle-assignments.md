# Lawyer Guide: Vehicle Assignments

Understand how you're assigned to vehicles and what access you receive.

---

## Overview

When you're assigned as legal counsel to a vehicle, you gain access to relevant documents and signing responsibilities.

---

## How Assignment Works

### Assignment Process

1. Arranger creates a vehicle
2. Configures service providers
3. Adds your firm as "Lawyer" or "Legal Counsel"
4. You're linked to the vehicle

### Notification

When assigned:
- Email notification
- Platform notification
- Vehicle appears in your list

---

## What You See

### Vehicle Information

For each assigned vehicle:
- Vehicle name and structure
- Jurisdiction
- Key contacts (arranger, admin)
- Associated deals

### Document Access

You can access:
- Constitutional documents
- Deal documents (for signing)
- Closing documents
- Relevant certificates

---

## Your Responsibilities

### For Each Vehicle

| Responsibility | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Certificate Signing** | Sign investor certificates |
| **Document Review** | Review as requested |
| **Closing Participation** | Sign at deal close |

### Ongoing Duties

- Respond to signature requests promptly
- Maintain access to the platform
- Update contact information if it changes

---

## Managing Multiple Vehicles

### Viewing All Assignments

1. Go to **Vehicles**
2. See all vehicles you're assigned to
3. Filter by status, jurisdiction

### Vehicle Details

Click on any vehicle to see:
- Full details
- Pending actions
- Document access
- Associated deals

---

## Changing Assignments

### If You're Replaced

If arranger assigns different counsel:
- You'll be notified
- Access removed
- Pending items transferred

### If You Need to Decline

Contact the arranger to:
- Discuss the assignment
- Arrange alternative counsel
- Handle transition
